About Me

I am a Biomedical engineer studying the cause and effects of seizures in epileptic patients. At my job I'm responsible for localization of seizure onset and 3D reconstruction of images for patient surgery.

I'm also involved in a large multi-site research study on Sudden unexpected death in epilepsy (SUDEP).
SUDEP is a fatal complication of epilepsy. It is defined as the sudden and unexpected, non-traumatic and non-drowning death of a person with epilepsy, without a toxicological or anatomical cause of death detected during the post-mortem examination. The work I do will help pave the way on finding a cure for epilepsy and to identify biomarkers for at risk patients.

The bootcamp course at Case Western Reserve University has taught me the tools necessary to build a surgical navigation application/database that will greatly help surgeons and hospital adminstation to track and monitor patient progress overtime.
